Four Pillars of Physical Wellness

Simple, sustainable support strategies for caregivers to implement every day.

Move Together

Plan 20–30 minute low-impact activities like neighborhood walks, chair stretches, or gentle dance. Consistency matters more than intensity.

  • Morning stretches after breakfast
  • 10-min neighborhood walk
  • Chair yoga 3× per week

Safer Home Setup

Reduce fall risks with simple modifications. Falls are the #1 cause of injury in older adults, most are preventable.

  • Non-slip bathroom mats
  • Night lights on all paths
  • Grab bars near toilet/tub

Nutrition Support

Build simple meal plans with protein, colorful vegetables, hydration reminders, and easy-prep snacks to avoid skipped meals.

  • Protein at every meal
  • Colorful vegetables daily
  • Water reminder every 2 hrs

Care Routine

Use a visible checklist for medications, checkups, and sleep routines. Invite elders to co-create the schedule for shared control.

  • Medication tracker chart
  • Regular checkup calendar
  • Consistent sleep schedule

Daily Movement Ideas

Activities matched to energy level and mobility, no gym required.

Low Energy

Gentle & Seated

  • Seated arm circles & shoulder rolls
  • Foot and ankle rotations
  • Neck stretches with deep breathing
  • Hand grip exercises with a soft ball
Moderate Energy

Active & Standing

  • Neighborhood walks (15–20 min)
  • Light garden or yard work
  • Standing kitchen stretches
  • Gentle dance to favorite music
Higher Energy

Active & Social

  • Community walking group
  • Water aerobics class
  • Swimming or light cycling
  • Tai chi or yoga class

Medication & Routine FAQs

Common questions caregivers face when managing daily health routines for older adults.

Source: AARP Caregiving Resource Center & NIH MedlinePlus, 2023

Use a shared pill organizer your elder can see and manage themselves. Set gentle phone reminders together. Ask "Did your morning pills happen?" rather than commanding. Offer to refill, not to supervise.

Watch for: unexplained weight loss, increased difficulty with stairs, new balance problems, reduced grip strength, complaints of fatigue during usual tasks, or changes in skin color/condition.

Batch-prep soft, protein-rich meals on weekends. Keep pre-cut fruit and easy snacks visible in the fridge. Eat together when possible, social meals increase appetite and enjoyment.

After any fall, hospitalization, or new diagnosis affecting mobility. Also consider a PT consult if you notice a new limp, fear of falling, or difficulty rising from chairs, these are treatable with early intervention.

Home Safety Checklist

Walk through the home together, use this as a shared project, not an inspection.

Entryways & Hallways
Clear clutter, secure rugs with double-sided tape, ensure adequate lighting at night.
Bathroom
Install grab bars near toilet and shower, use non-slip mat, set water heater below 120°F to prevent scalding.
Stairs & Steps
Both-side handrails, non-slip stair treads, motion-sensor lighting for night trips.
Bedroom
Bed at the right height for easy transfer, phone within reach, nightlight on path to bathroom.
