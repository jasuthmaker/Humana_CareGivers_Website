Social Connection Across Generations

Connection is care: shared time helps older adults feel valued and less isolated.

Simple social moments—calls, stories, games, and community events—can strengthen emotional wellness for both teens and elders.

Start the connection checklist

Intergenerational Activities That Build Belonging

Story Swap

Record a short “memory interview.” Ask about first job, favorite music, or family traditions.

Tech Buddy Time

Teach one digital skill each week: video calling, photo sharing, reminders, or scam awareness basics.

Community Together

Attend local events, faith gatherings, or community center programs to increase social participation.

Shared Hobby Hour

Try gardening, board games, cooking, or music practice. Focus on enjoyment, not performance.

Weekly Connection Checklist

  • One in-person or video conversation focused on listening.
  • One shared activity that includes movement or creativity.
  • One social touchpoint with neighbors, family, or community group.
  • One gratitude message: “I’m glad we did this together.”
